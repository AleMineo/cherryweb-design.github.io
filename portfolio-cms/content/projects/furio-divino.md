<div class="description">
<h4>01 — Brand Concept & Meaning</h4>

The identity revolves around the idea of elevating wine to something almost sacred — a refined pleasure, worthy of celebration.<br>
The logo visually transforms a stylized red wine glass into a symbolic icon.

Key conceptual elements include:

<ul>
<li>A minimal red wine glass as the central symbol</li>
<li>A golden-yellow circle forming the rim of the glass, evoking a halo</li>
<li>Two stylized wings extending from the sides of the stem</li>
</ul>

The halo and wings subtly suggest the idea of something “divine,” reinforcing the emotional and sensory elevation associated with wine tasting.
</div>

<div class="description">
<h4>02 — Typography & Wordplay</h4>

The lettering “furio diVino” is designed using a handwritten style to reflect personality, authenticity, and direct human connection — essential traits for a sommelier.<br>

The colored emphasis on “di” creates a clever double meaning:

<ul>
<li><strong>di-vino</strong> (of wine)</li>
<li><strong>divino</strong> (divine)</li>
</ul>

This typographic intervention strengthens the conceptual bridge between wine culture and the idea of something heavenly or exceptional.
</div>

<div class="description">
<h4>03 — Visual Balance & Style</h4>

The design balances irony and elegance.<br>
While the symbolic halo and wings introduce a playful narrative layer, the overall composition remains clean and recognizable, allowing the logo to adapt well across digital and print applications.

The stylization ensures:

<ul>
<li>Immediate recognizability</li>
<li>Strong silhouette and icon potential</li>
<li>Versatility for labels, social media, and promotional materials</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="description">
